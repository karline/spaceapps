Our Mission:

Use crowdsourced biometric data to optimize astronaut health in space and get humans healthier on Earth – so they can head for the stars.

The prestige of being chosen for a NASA space mission comes with risks. Astronauts may endure a variety of adverse health effects during their mission, often due to changes in gravity, motion, and radiation. While NASA’s health monitoring is rigorous, it has a problem: Astronauts are a select few – a small sample set primarily composed of elite athletes. For that reason, it may be difficult for NASA to identify health outliers that pose risks during long-term missions.

For NASA

Elevate is a health monitoring and crowdsourcing application which adds rich data to NASA’s knowledge of astronaut health. Astronaut health data will be steamed into the application’s portal, and this information will be viewable by the NASA command team and by astronauts themselves. During missions, astronaut biometric data will be compared against previous astronauts and against crowdsourced “earthlings” with similar biometric markers to enable data-driven predictions and recommendations. This broader data set will allow NASA to more easily identify markers that are outside of norm.

For Users

For crowd sourced users, Elevate provides a way not only to help NASA, but also to track their own biomarkers against other users with similar height and weight markers. In addition, the app lets users track their fitness against a NASA health metric to determine how close they are to being “as fit as an astronaut.”

For the Future

Elevate also allows NASA to track the crowdsourced data against astronaut biomarkers. As this data set grows, it will allow NASA to discover the boundaries for “normal” in the general population, and how that differs from astronauts with similar height and weight statistics. By mining this data, we can discover what a candidate who can become “space-ready” looks like on Earth. This crowdsourcing component will not only improve public knowledge about astronaut health, but it will also enable NASA to screen for humans who are “fit enough” to be astronauts. If space colonization is to become feasible, a robust health monitoring and analysis tool is essential. Elevate is designed to help bring the human race to the next level of space travel.
